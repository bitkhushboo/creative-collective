The Creative Collective

Project Overview

▼

In February 2023, India Docs organised a three-day, in-person Content Creator Summit - a gathering of some of the most effective content creators in India. One of the problem statements identified by the creators at our last event was: How to make investigative journalism accessible through social media content?

In an attempt to answer this question, we are organizing our second gathering next month. We will bring together journalists, film editors, producers, and content creators over 5 days to brainstorm on two videos.

The Creative Collective Project

▼

Our plan is to extend these brainstorming gatherings over the coming year, with the primary goal of integrating investigative and narrative journalism into social media and content creation. Each gathering will focus on two compelling news stories, transforming them into dramatic and engaging videos. The videos will have uniform artistic language and branding under the umbrella of a loose collective that will have fluid entry and exit for talents with specific skill sets. India docs will remain the core organizers for consistency and accountability purposes.

Project Proposal

▼

We plan to create at least four additional videos, spread across two gatherings, beginning in January and then June respectively. The budget for the same is attached at the end.

The focus of the next gathering will be on the following 2 videos:

  1. Youth: A comprehensive exploration of issues affecting young people in India, weaving together narratives on unemployment, NEET and other entrance exam scams, radicalization, mental health challenges, and drug proliferation. This video will aim to provide a holistic view of the challenges faced by India's youth and potential solutions.
  2. Economic inequality: A visually striking video using various infographics and visual aids to illustrate the unimaginable extent of inequality in India. We want to produce a 20-minute video on wealth inequality in India, with emphasis on scale using visual aids like infographics. One of our favorite examples is how Neil deGrasse Tyson explained the scale of time and history from the Big Bang until now using a year. Additionally, it would be interesting to delve into what such inequality means in market and progress terms, or even environmentally.

Tangible Outcomes

▼
  • Two longform videos that will bring attention to specific issues that engender civic consciousness and deepen democratic responsibilities. The best example of this is Dhruv Rathee who has been able to create critical mass around significant issues through the use of discursive logic and narrative journalism.
  • Short and engaging reels on Instagram in the form of Micro-Content Video Essays/Reels - This is a format of video essaying and micro-content creation that is using an "infodump" tactic to sprinkle progressive content into the Instagram reel scroll. These formats mimic the structure and aesthetics of "slice of life" or "motivation" podcast promotion reels to present journalistic or academic content around the themes of economics, law, sociology or geopolitics etc.

Examples of this type of content are:

  1. Johnny Harris is an early pioneer of this format.
  2. @micheal__mezz makes video essays in bitable formats in edits showing graphs, news headlines and memes that also gets uploaded to bleed into Instagram scrolling.
  3. @african_stream uses podcasting production and documentary film making clippings along with snappy fast-paced edits to talk about geopolitics in Africa.

Intangible Outcomes

▼
  • Building on our previous work of organizing content creators. Adding to the already existing cohort.
  • Brainstorming even more content experiments with the cohort.
  • How to organize / foster collaboration.
  • Increase visibility, more creators, more topics.
  • Improve capacity, do it more consistently.
  • Holding space, mental health checks.

Goal

▼

We would aim for 1 million views on each video and have allocated personnel and budget accordingly.

Previous Meeting Outcomes

▼

Networking and Community Building

  • Several creators reported that the Creator Summit was the first time for them to have met each other even though they have often interacted with each other online.
  • In addition to everyone invited, two creators who have anonymous accounts (Nimo Yadav and Rofl Gandhi) heard about the event through attendees and joined us at the event.
  • The content creators were largely independent actors before the Summit. The gathering was an opportunity to meet and strategize.
  • Many attendees said they didn't know how much they needed something like this to happen. The isolation that comes with an unstructured and novel pursuit as independent content creation was making them vulnerable to burnout.
  • After the Summit, attendees reported feeling like they weren't operating alone in a vacuum and found a community to fall back on since their work comes with a lot of threats, blowback and potentially hostile uncertainty.
  • They self-created a WhatsApp group that became the central node for organizing around the Broadcast Bill campaign - as self-reported by several cohort creators.
  • Balram had shut down the Andheri West Shit Posting account he used to operate. He told us that the mental toll of trolling and hate speech against him of the kind that the OP India piece against him in 2021 brought to him (amongst the otherwise ever-present hate speech and trolling) led him to shut down this page.1 After the Summit, Balram told the India Docs team that he regained the confidence to start his page again.2

Collaborations / Provocations3

  • Two content creators collaborated during the gathering on two small skits. The videos were created and uploaded on X (formerly Twitter) and Instagram.
  • A journalist, Anuj and Meghnad have agreed to pursue making several content pieces about an RTI investigation conducted by Anuj about drone technology in civil policing, its weaponizing capabilities and facial recognition capabilities.
  • Satvik started streaming on YouTube after the summit. Meghnad, a seasoned streamer, helped him set up and structure his streams.
  • Meghnad, Satvik and Ramit are collaborating on a potential documentary about democracy and are going on a recce trip to Bihar, a state in eastern India.
  • Ramit, Kartik and Kunal are also collaborating to create audio-visual journalistic content.
